BikeStore Sales Performance Analysis

End-to-End Business Analytics Case Study using SQL & Power BI

Advanced Retail Analytics • Recruiter-Ready

Business Problem

A bike retail company wanted a clear understanding of sales performance, discount impact, category contribution, and store efficiency to support data-driven business decisions.

  • Which products and categories drive maximum revenue?
  • How do discounts impact net revenue and margins?
  • Which stores and staff contribute most to sales?
  • Are there seasonal and weekly sales patterns?

Dataset & Data Model

The analysis is based on a real-world BikeStore retail dataset consisting of 9 relational tables designed using a star-schema approach.

  • Fact Table: order_items (transaction-level sales)
  • Dimensions: customers, products, categories, brands, stores, staff, orders
  • Grain: Order-item level

Power BI Dashboard

SQL-validated metrics are transformed into an interactive Power BI dashboard to support quick, data-driven decision-making.

  • KPI cards for revenue, orders, customers, discounts
  • Monthly and weekly revenue trends
  • Category, product, store & staff performance

Key Business Insights

  • Mountain Bikes generate the highest revenue but also face margin risk due to heavy discounts.
  • Revenue is concentrated in a limited set of products, indicating dependency risk.
  • Baldwin Bikes outperforms other stores in both revenue and order volume.
  • Sales show strong seasonality with specific peak months.
  • Weekends and Mondays outperform mid-week sales.

Business Recommendations

  • Apply category-specific discount strategies to protect margins.
  • Expand high-profit categories to reduce revenue concentration risk.
  • Leverage top-performing staff practices across stores.
  • Run targeted campaigns during high-performing seasons and days.

What I Didn’t Do & Why

  • Sales forecasting – future data was unavailable.
  • Customer lifetime value – repeat purchase lifecycle data was missing.
  • Inventory optimization – stock movement analysis was out of scope.

Conclusion

This project demonstrates an end-to-end retail analytics workflow — from SQL-based validation to Power BI visualization and executive-level insights — reflecting a strong business-oriented analytical mindset.
